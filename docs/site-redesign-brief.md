# Website Redesign Brief

*Last updated: 2026-03-25*

## Objective
Redesign the site so it attracts and converts nontechnical founders and business owners who need:

- an MVP built quickly
- workflow automation that removes manual work
- AI agents that solve a real operational problem

The site should behave like a focused services business, not a general developer portfolio.

## Diagnosis
The current site is trying to do too many jobs at once:

- personal brand site
- developer portfolio
- blog
- productized launch offer
- AI engineer positioning

That creates a clarity problem. A nontechnical founder or business owner should understand the offer in five seconds. Right now the homepage headline, navigation, and section order make the visitor do too much interpretation.

The current visual style is distinctive, but the message hierarchy is still portfolio-first. "Fullstack + AI Engineer" describes you, not the client outcome. The launch page is stronger commercially, but it is isolated instead of being the main spine of the site.

## Positioning Recommendation
### Core promise
"I build MVPs and workflow automation systems for nontechnical founders and business owners."

### Secondary promise
"You get direct senior execution, plain-English collaboration, and a faster path from idea or manual process to working software."

### Primary CTA
`Book a Roadmap Call`

### Secondary CTA
`See Relevant Case Studies`

## Information Architecture Recommendation
### Primary navigation
- Home
- MVP Builds
- Automation & AI Agents
- Case Studies
- About
- Insights
- Book a Call

### Pages to de-emphasize
- `Now`
- timeline-style experience anchors in the main nav
- broad "Projects" as a generic bucket

### Why
Visitors with buying intent do not need a general browse path first. They need:

- a clear "what you do"
- proof that you have done it
- a simple next step

## Homepage Recommendation
### 1. Hero
Replace identity-led framing with audience-and-outcome framing.

Recommended direction:
- Headline: "MVPs and AI automation for nontechnical founders and business owners."
- Subhead: "I turn product ideas and manual workflows into working software without agency overhead or technical hand-holding."
- Primary CTA: `Book a Roadmap Call`
- Secondary CTA: `See Case Studies`

### 2. Fit filter
Very early on the page, say who it is for and who it is not for.

Good-fit examples:
- founders who need a real v1 shipped
- businesses buried in manual workflow
- teams that want useful AI, not demos

Bad-fit examples:
- idea-only projects
- giant enterprise scopes
- teams shopping for the cheapest dev hours

### 3. Two core offer cards
Feature two offers only:

- `MVP Builds`
- `Workflow Automation & AI Agents`

Do not lead with "feature sprints" on the homepage. That offer attracts a more technical buyer and weakens the service focus you want.

### 4. Proof strip
Move proof much closer to the top:

- years of experience
- notable companies
- shipped products
- one strong testimonial

### 5. Case studies by problem type
Do not present case studies as a flat portfolio grid first. Group them by business problem:

- launching a new product
- simplifying a workflow
- adding AI functionality that users actually use

### 6. Process section
Keep the scoping-to-launch structure from the current launch page, but adapt it for both MVP and automation engagements.

### 7. Objection handling
Answer the questions a nontechnical buyer actually has:

- How do we decide what to build first?
- How much do I need to prepare?
- Will this work if I am not technical?
- What makes AI worth it here?

### 8. Final CTA
End with one conversion path only: the roadmap call.

## Visual Direction Recommendation
Keep the site distinctive, but shift the tone from "developer portfolio" to "high-trust operator."

- Reduce emphasis on hacker/developer self-labeling.
- Increase emphasis on business outcomes, proof, and process.
- Use fewer competing sections on the homepage.
- Make the CTA path visible above the fold and repeated after proof sections.
- Keep personality, but make it easier for a nontechnical buyer to immediately understand what they are buying.

## Content Recommendation
### Keep
- launch/process language that speaks to speed and direct execution
- testimonials
- selected case studies
- blog for SEO and credibility

### Rewrite
- homepage hero
- services labels and descriptions
- homepage section order
- CTA copy

### Reposition
- "Fullstack AI Engineer" becomes supporting context, not the headline
- "The 4-Week Launch" becomes either the MVP service page or the commercial backbone of the home page

## Suggested Rollout
### Phase 1
- Rewrite homepage
- Simplify navigation
- Make the CTA consistent
- Add two service pages

### Phase 2
- Rewrite case studies around outcomes, not just projects
- Add FAQ and qualification language
- Improve attribution and analytics quality

### Phase 3
- Tighten blog-to-service conversion paths
- Add industry- or workflow-specific landing pages if needed

## Decision Rule
If a section does not help a nontechnical founder or business owner answer one of these questions, it should be cut or demoted:

- What do you do?
- Is this for me?
- Can I trust you?
- What happens next?
