# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a local command-center style: calm, structured, and operational. The video should feel like a working business system, not a generic SaaS promo. Use the public Acquire product screenshot as the proof anchor, then surround it with clear cards, rails, approval markers, and evidence chips. The copy stays simple and nontechnical: problem, cost, solution, how it works, result, and why it matters.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#eef2ed`
- Panel: `#fbfcf8`
- Ink: `#15201b`
- Muted text: `#55645d`
- Border: `#d3ddd5`
- Accent green: `#1d6f58`
- Accent soft: `#dfeee6`
- Approval amber: `#c7862f`
- Deep slate: `#17231e`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Six scenes, `39s` total, with each scene held for `6.5s`.
- Use quiet but visible motion: cards assemble, rails reveal, screenshot settles, evidence chips enter.
- Use push transitions for the first three handoffs, then blur crossfade into the result, then a gentler push into the final frame.
- Add ambient motion to background panels and grid so the frame does not feel static.
- Keep product-screenshot scenes readable for the full hold.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show private runtime data, resumes, outreach drafts, account information, local database rows, browser sessions, tokens, or file paths.
- Do not put stack names, CLI commands, database names, prompt names, or test output on screen.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
- Do not make the video a title-card sequence only; the public product surface must carry proof.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
