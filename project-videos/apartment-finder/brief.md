# Project Video Brief

Project: Apartment Finder

Video type: Short AI proof demo

Target duration: 36 seconds

Primary viewer: Hiring manager or general portfolio viewer

Story mode: Short demo

One-sentence job: Apartment Finder turns noisy rental listing data into an evidence-backed washer decision the user can review.

Viewer takeaway: This is an AI product workflow that makes a messy apartment-search decision easier to trust.

Plain story:

- Problem: apartment sites blur private in-unit washers and shared building laundry into the same search signal.
- Solution: the product scans listing details and photos, then shows the evidence behind the washer decision.
- Result: the user can compare likely in-unit, shared-building, and unclear results without opening every listing by hand.

Scene arc:

1. Problem: apartment search got noisy.
2. Cost: the real answer lived across photos and details.
3. Solution: Apartment Finder reviews the listing evidence.
4. How it works: the AI decision stays inspectable.
5. Result: results become easier to compare.
6. Why it matters: the search turns into a clearer decision.

Reading-speed constraint:

- Use one headline plus one short sentence at most.
- Keep most scenes below 15 must-read words.
- Hold each scene for about 5.5 seconds.
- Keep implementation terms off-screen; evidence and decision words can appear.

Assets needed:

- Screenshot: redacted product surface made from the public Apartment Finder UI image.
- UI clip: not needed; motion is handled in HyperFrames around static and synthetic product surfaces.
- Artifact: synthetic listing cards, photo evidence cards, and decision cards.
- Diagram/data point: simple evidence-to-decision rail, not technical architecture.

Privacy notes:

- Hide: private addresses, listing URLs, listing IDs, contacts, raw source listings, cached photos, local paths, tokens, and runtime data.
- Safe to show: public project name, redacted public UI screenshot, synthetic listing rows, synthetic evidence cards, decision labels, and case-study metrics used only as backing proof.
