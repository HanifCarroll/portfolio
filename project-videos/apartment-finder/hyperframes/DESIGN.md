# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a focused evidence-review style: calm, precise, and product-led. The video should feel like a compact decision workspace, not a real-estate ad. Use the redacted Apartment Finder product surface as the proof anchor, then surround it with synthetic listing cards, photo evidence cards, confidence markers, and decision chips. Keep copy simple and nontechnical: noisy search, photo evidence, AI review, inspectable decision, clearer comparison.

## Concept Angle

The video turns a noisy apartment hunt into a review bench: listing cards enter as uncertain inputs, evidence gets inspected, and the final decision becomes visible enough to trust.

## Motion

- Six scenes plus a `3s` held end card, `36s` total.
- Primary transitions are push slides for story progression.
- Use a blur crossfade when moving from product surface to evidence review.
- Cards should enter with varied direction, opacity, scale, and slight rotation.
- Product surface scenes should hold long enough to inspect.
- Add ambient grid/panel drift across the whole video.
- The final end card holds cleanly from `33s` to `36s`.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show real listing URLs, IDs, addresses, or source row details.
- Do not use raw cached listing photos from the adjacent project repo.
- Do not show stack names, model names, CLI commands, API names, or test output on screen.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
- Do not make the video a title-card sequence only; the redacted product surface must carry proof.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
