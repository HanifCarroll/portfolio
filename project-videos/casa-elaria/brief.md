# Project Video Brief

Project: Casa Elaria

Video type: Fake-door validation loop

Target duration: 21 seconds

Primary viewer: Hiring manager or general portfolio viewer

Story mode: Short demo

One-sentence job: Casa Elaria tested whether visitors would act like buyers before the founders spent on inventory.

Viewer takeaway: This is a focused validation build that turned a product idea into a demand signal.

Plain story:

- Problem: the founders had a beauty product idea, but inventory would require real spend.
- Solution: a believable storefront let visitors browse, choose, and reach the buying moment.
- Result: checkout captured interest instead of payment, creating demand signal before inventory.

Scene arc:

1. Product idea: a beauty concept needed proof before inventory.
2. Storefront test: the site looked and behaved like a real store.
3. Fake-door moment: checkout captured launch-list interest instead of payment.
4. Demand signal: product intent and emails showed what to stock first.
5. Ending beat: Casa Elaria, demand before inventory.

Reading-speed constraint:

- Keep each scene to one idea.
- Use one headline plus one short sentence at most.
- Hold the final frame for 3 seconds.
- Keep implementation terms and stack names off-screen.

Assets needed:

- Screenshot: public Casa Elaria storefront hero.
- Screenshot: public Casa Elaria product detail page.
- UI clip: not needed; motion is built in HyperFrames around static public assets.
- Artifact: native cards for checkout interest and demand signal.
- Diagram/data point: simple validation loop, not technical architecture.

Privacy notes:

- Hide: client-sensitive operating details, private analytics, emails, accounts, tokens, cookies, and local paths.
- Safe to show: public portfolio screenshots, public project name, plain validation labels, and native synthetic signal cards.
