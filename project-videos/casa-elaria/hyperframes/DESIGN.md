# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a warm validation-storefront style: cream paper, deep olive, soap-label gold, ink text, public Casa Elaria screenshots, and quiet ecommerce motion. The video should feel like a product validation loop, not a generic launch ad. Keep copy plain and short: product idea, storefront test, checkout interest, demand signal, and final inventory decision.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#f5f1e8`
- Paper: `#fffaf1`
- Ink: `#211f18`
- Muted text: `#665f50`
- Border: `#d9cfbd`
- Olive: `#304b10`
- Olive soft: `#e5ead8`
- Gold: `#b88a35`
- Clay: `#a35f42`
- Deep frame: `#1f2717`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Five beats over `21s`: four short story scenes plus a `3s` ending hold.
- Use quiet push transitions and small scale settles around the public screenshots.
- Keep screenshots readable after entry.
- Use cards and rails only to clarify the fake-door loop.
- The final `3s` should hold cleanly with no new argument.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not make the video a generic ecommerce ad.
- Do not show implementation stack names, command output, analytics internals, payment provider names, or test counts on screen.
- Do not invent private customer data or fake numerical results.
- Do not show real emails, accounts, tokens, cookies, billing details, or local paths.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
