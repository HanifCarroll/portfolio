# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a calm stay-decision product style that fits Casamo: bright furnished-stay imagery, warm off-white surfaces, deep navy product framing, teal trust accents, and restrained report cards. The video should feel useful and founder-owned, not like client work or a generic travel ad. Keep the copy plain and structured around the product walkthrough: problem, evidence, intake, audit, result, and product loop.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#F4F2EA`
- Paper: `#FFFCF4`
- Ink: `#10263B`
- Muted text: `#5D6A66`
- Border: `#D8DED5`
- Navy: `#071F3A`
- Teal: `#0D9DA3`
- Teal soft: `#DDF3F0`
- Leaf: `#356B50`
- Sand: `#E8D8BF`
- Amber: `#B7792E`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Medium-energy product walkthrough pacing.
- Use push slides for adjacent workflow steps, blur crossfades for audit/result reveals, and a soft final fade after the ending beat.
- Every scene enters with varied opacity, transform, rule reveal, and card/image motion.
- Keep product images steady after entry so the viewer can inspect them.
- Use native cards only for real workflow concepts: trip needs, evidence states, report outputs, and product loop.
- Keep ambient motion subtle: slow grid drift, small panel movement, and gentle proof-image settling.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not call Hanif a client or frame Casamo as client work.
- Do not show implementation stack names, API names, background job names, test output, command output, or private system details.
- Do not show private accounts, customer data, payments, analytics, tokens, local paths, or non-public listing data.
- Do not make the video a generic travel inspiration reel; keep it anchored to decision support and the product workflow.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
