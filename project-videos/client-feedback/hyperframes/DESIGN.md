# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a warm evidence-lab style: light paper canvas, dark ink, teal source rails, blue email accents, amber artifact receipts, and one dark proof surface. The piece should feel like a practical local workflow, not a generic SaaS promo. The copy stays simple and nontechnical: scattered feedback, broken trail, local packet, saved artifacts, reusable proof.

Concept angle: private feedback moves from scattered personal channels into a sealed local packet that can be reviewed later.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#F2EEE6`
- Paper: `#FFFCF6`
- Ink: `#17211E`
- Muted text: `#5F665F`
- Border: `#D6CCBD`
- Source teal: `#167A6F`
- Source teal soft: `#DDEEE8`
- Email blue: `#2754D8`
- Artifact amber: `#B8682D`
- Deep proof panel: `#111A24`

## Typography

- Display/body: `Montserrat`, sans-serif
- Data/labels: `IBM Plex Mono`, monospace

## Motion

- Six beats, `31.5s` total.
- Use push slides between adjacent story beats and a blur crossfade into the final result.
- Use varied entrances: copy from the left, cards from mixed directions, rails by scale reveals, packet cards by stagger.
- Add subtle ambient motion to the background grid, edge panels, and ghost label.
- Keep the final card settled for 2.5-3 seconds before the fade.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show private feedback text, contact info, emails, recordings, transcript text, local paths, tokens, cookies, browser storage, or OAuth state.
- Do not put real message screenshots or mailbox rows on screen.
- Do not make command syntax the main story.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, dark-blue SaaS styling, or decorative blobs.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
