# Design

## Style Prompt

Use an industrial catalog style that fits an auto-parts stockroom and the public Desarmadero Latorre website: warm workshop texture, clean catalog cards, dark steel framing, and the site's bright blue search accent. The video should feel practical and operational, not like a generic product promo. The proof anchor is the public catalog screenshot; motion should clarify the workflow from phone-first questions to search, listing context, and contact.

## Concept Angle

The video treats the catalog as a digital stockroom window: customers move from vague stock calls to a visible search surface and a more specific part question.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#f3f1ec`
- Surface: `#fffdf8`
- Ink: `#20211f`
- Muted text: `#62635f`
- Steel: `#30454d`
- Steel dark: `#24343a`
- Search blue: `#55bfe8`
- Search soft: `#d9f3fb`
- Workshop amber: `#b47a48`
- Border: `#d8d3ca`

## Typography

- Display: `Oswald`, condensed sans
- Body: `Montserrat`, geometric sans
- Labels/data: `Space Mono`, monospace

The pairing uses industrial signage for headlines, a clear catalog UI voice for body copy, and monospace labels for workflow metadata.

## Foreground Detail Plan

- Registration marks and thin steel rules around the frame.
- A small scene number and workflow label in the top corner.
- Search-field callouts for make, model, and year.
- Listing context chips for photo, year, vehicle, and details.
- A bottom workflow rail that ends on contact with a specific question.

## Motion

- `21s` total.
- Four compact workflow beats plus one ending beat.
- Push transitions between workflow steps.
- Blur crossfade into the end card.
- Product screenshots stay readable after entrance.
- Ambient motion stays subtle: slow grid drift, photo parallax, and small callout reveals.

## Constraints

- Use only public portfolio assets and synthetic labels.
- Keep text simple and readable.
- Keep each scene to one idea.
- Keep the final ending beat settled for roughly three seconds.
