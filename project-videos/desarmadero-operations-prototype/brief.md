# Project Video Brief

Project: Desarmadero Operations Prototype

Video type: Discovery-to-prototype case-study video

Target duration: 45 seconds

Primary viewer: Hiring manager or general portfolio viewer

Story mode: Plain overview

One-sentence job: Desarmadero Operations Prototype turns one discovery call about a yard operation into a clickable product model the client can validate before the full build.

Viewer takeaway: This is forward-deployed product engineering: understand the real workflow, build a working model, and make the next scope decision clearer.

Plain story:

- Problem: the yard operation was spread across paper budget sheets, WhatsApp, Excel, and memory.
- Solution: one discovery call became a PRD, functional spec, and role-based clickable prototype.
- Result: the client could validate the operating model through a working app before committing to the full scope.

Scene arc:

1. Problem: the yard ran on paper, messages, spreadsheets, and memory.
2. Cost: every handoff had to stay aligned across sale, payment, assignment, and yard location.
3. Solution: one discovery call became a clickable prototype.
4. How it works: seller, cash desk, manager, and dismantler each get a focused surface.
5. Result: the 301-position yard map makes cars searchable by plate.
6. Why it matters: the client could react to a working model before a full quote.

Reading-speed constraint:

- Use one headline plus one short sentence at most.
- Keep stack names, repo terms, and credentials off-screen.
- Hold the real prototype screenshots long enough to inspect.
- Reserve the final 2.5-3 seconds for the project name and one short result line.

Assets needed:

- Screenshot: public counter-sales prototype screen from the portfolio case study.
- Screenshot: public yard-map prototype screen from the portfolio case study.
- Artifact: native cards for discovery call, PRD, functional spec, role surfaces, and scope alignment.
- Diagram/data point: simple handoff rail and 301-position map callout.

Privacy notes:

- Hide: discovery transcript contents, any private client details, credentials, repository internals, local paths, account state, tokens, and raw data.
- Safe to show: public anonymized screenshots, public project name, general workflow roles, and synthetic/native HyperFrames labels.
