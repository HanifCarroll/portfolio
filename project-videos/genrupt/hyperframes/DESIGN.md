# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a flagship AI systems case-study style that feels commercial, precise, and understandable. The video should look like a production product surface moving from AI media into paid seller-team operations. Use Genrupt's light interface screenshots as the proof layer, then add stronger video-native structure: glassy panels, circuit-line texture, restrained gradient accents, status rails, credit cards, allowed-workflow cards, and one clear result card. The copy stays plain: problem, stakes, solution, commercial foundation, agent access, result.

## Typography

- Display: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Six scenes plus a 3-second ending beat, `45s` total.
- Use medium-energy product-systems pacing: push slides for adjacent story points, blur crossfades for major shifts, and a calm final fade.
- Every scene starts with entrance animation on the headline, proof surface, and native cards.
- Keep screenshots readable after entry; native overlays should frame the proof, not cover it.
- Use ambient circuit-line drift and small status pulses so the product frames feel alive without becoming a generic tech promo.
- Hold the final result card long enough for the first-100-customers line to land.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show account names, profile photos, private account footer data, provider names, routes, IDs, command output, test counts, secrets, tokens, cookies, or file paths.
- Do not make the video a title-card sequence only; product screenshots must carry visible proof.
- Do not make the palette one-note purple or generic neon-on-dark.
- Do not show stack names, schema names, API endpoints, or internal job mechanics on screen.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
