# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a local product MVP style: crisp white product surfaces, soft neighborhood-map structure, navy interface text, blue action accents, and green coordination markers. The video should feel practical and trustworthy, like a working local platform rather than a social-media promo. Keep the story focused on problem, product flow, and result: practice is hard to make real, HablaBA centers small local exchanges, users explore and coordinate, and the MVP loop is ready to test.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#f4f7fb`
- Panel: `#ffffff`
- Ink: `#101827`
- Muted text: `#52627a`
- Border: `#dce4ef`
- Action blue: `#215dff`
- Action soft: `#eaf0ff`
- Local green: `#1f7a5d`
- Local soft: `#e4f4ed`
- Amber note: `#bf7a1f`
- Deep navy: `#111827`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Six timed beats, `30s` total.
- Use quiet product motion: disconnected cards assemble, screenshots slide and settle, exchange details reveal, chat proof appears, and the final loop locks.
- Keep screenshot scenes readable after the first second.
- Use push/dissolve transitions without jump cuts.
- Hold the final message for a settled `2.5-3s` ending beat.

## What Not To Do

- Do not show stack names, API endpoints, test output, command output, or local paths.
- Do not turn the video into a generic dating-app or social-network promo.
- Do not rely only on title cards; the public HablaBA product surfaces must carry proof.
- Do not zoom into profile photos, account controls, or usernames.
- Do not use purple-blue gradients, neon styling, or a one-note blue palette.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
