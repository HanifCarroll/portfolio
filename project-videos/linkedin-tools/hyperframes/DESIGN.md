# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a controlled operations-room style for a guarded automation system: light review surfaces, deep slate navigation accents, green safety markers, amber warning rails, and crisp evidence cards. The video should feel like a working workflow controller, not a generic automation promo. The copy stays plain: problem, ownership, controllers, boundaries, evidence, and why the system matters.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#EEF3F6`
- Surface: `#FFFFFF`
- Soft surface: `#F7FAFC`
- Ink: `#111827`
- Muted text: `#52627A`
- Border: `#CBD6E2`
- Safety green: `#1C7C5B`
- Safety soft: `#DDF4E8`
- Warning amber: `#B87513`
- Warning soft: `#FFF2CF`
- Deep slate: `#172331`
- Deep slate soft: `#24364A`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Six story scenes plus a settled end card, `45s` total.
- Use push transitions and controlled evidence-card reveals.
- Product screenshot scenes should hold long enough to inspect the review UI.
- Guardrail rails should lock into place before send-capable labels appear.
- Evidence cards should assemble like an audit trail, not like decorative feature tiles.
- Keep ambient movement low-amplitude: background grid drift, panel shifts, and line reveals.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show private LinkedIn runtime data, browser profiles, messages, profile names, account state, tokens, cookies, credentials, or local state paths.
- Do not make implementation terms the main message.
- Do not show full CLI commands or test counts on screen.
- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
- Do not turn the video into abstract title cards only; the review UI must carry proof.
- Do not use crowded copy or multiple competing ideas in one scene.
