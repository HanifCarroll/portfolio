# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a restrained interior-design portfolio loop that feels close to the Maximo site itself: black marble, clean white navigation, muted red accents, warm brass highlights, and rich interior imagery. The video should feel premium and quiet, with the real site screenshots carrying the proof. Copy stays minimal and source-faithful: public home, work-first presentation, inquiry path, and final project result.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#090807`
- Ink: `#f5f1ea`
- Muted text: `#c8beb4`
- Panel: `#f7f4ef`
- Panel ink: `#171513`
- Border: `#d8d0c8`
- Accent red: `#b65b55`
- Brass: `#d1a85a`
- Foliage: `#506b42`
- Deep shadow: `#020202`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Four scenes, `18s` total.
- Use calm blur crossfades between scenes, matching the premium portfolio mood.
- Use slow screenshot pushes and contained vertical movement to create a site-scroll loop from static public assets.
- Keep overlays quiet and readable; the real site imagery should remain the visual lead.
- Hold the final result frame for the last `3s` without new motion.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not show stack names, command output, local paths, or test counts.
- Do not invent client metrics or inquiry volume.
- Do not cover the interior imagery with heavy copy.
- Do not add neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic SaaS cards.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
