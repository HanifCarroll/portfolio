# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a clear social-product engineering style: pale mint canvas, dark ink, Mucho green, coral action accents, and crisp product screenshots. The video should feel like a concise engineering contribution clip, not a generic marketing reel. Keep copy plain and readable: problem, contribution, core workflows, team patterns, result, ending beat.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#EFF8F5`
- Panel: `#FFFFFF`
- Ink: `#111827`
- Muted text: `#52606D`
- Border: `#CFE4DC`
- Brand green: `#147A60`
- Brand soft: `#DDF3EB`
- Deep green: `#0A3F34`
- Coral action: `#F8334E`
- Purple tag: `#A855F7`
- Gold note: `#D29A2E`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Short clip pacing: five story scenes plus a held end card.
- Use push/fade transitions, screenshot settles, card rails, and chip reveals.
- Keep product screenshots steady after entry so the viewer can inspect them.
- Keep every scene to one idea and hold the final card for `2.5-3s`.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not describe the client field as a role title.
- Do not show stack names, API names, schema names, test output, command output, private team notes, credentials, tokens, or local paths.
- Do not make the video a title-card sequence only; the public product screenshots must carry proof.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
- Do not use purple-blue gradients, neon styling, or dense technical diagrams.
