# Project Video Brief

Project: Palabruno

Video type: Plain overview video

Target duration: 39-40 seconds

Primary viewer: Hiring manager or general portfolio viewer

Story mode: Plain overview

One-sentence job: Palabruno turns a founder's Spanish-reading idea into a launched mobile and web product for learners and teachers.

Viewer takeaway: This is a product build that turned a real founder idea into a launch-ready business surface with a clearer first buyer.

Plain story:

- Problem: the founder had a strong idea, but the first commercial version and buyer path needed shape.
- Solution: one focused product split the learner reader from the teacher workspace.
- Result: the founder left with iOS, Android, web, payments, launch assets, and handoff docs.

Scene arc:

1. Problem: the idea was strong, but the MVP shape was the harder question.
2. Cost: a student reader alone was useful, while teachers had the clearer repeated need.
3. Solution: Palabruno became two connected surfaces for learners and teachers.
4. How it works: teachers create readings, manage students, organize groups, and share lesson material.
5. Result: the launch included apps, web, payments, store assets, and handoff docs.
6. Why it matters: the founder could test the product with real Spanish teachers.

Reading-speed constraint:

- Use one headline plus one short sentence at most.
- Keep most scenes below 15 must-read words.
- Hold each scene for roughly 6-7 seconds.
- Keep implementation terms out of the main on-screen copy.

Assets needed:

- Screenshot: public case-study product composite.
- Screenshot: teacher create-reading workspace.
- Screenshot: teacher student/group management.
- Screenshot: teacher recent-share or library state.
- UI clip: not needed; motion is handled in HyperFrames around static public assets.
- Artifact: simple launch-surface cards built natively in HyperFrames.
- Diagram/data point: connected learner/teacher surface map built natively in HyperFrames.

Privacy notes:

- Hide: runtime accounts, private founder/client data, real student contact details, billing/account details, tokens, and local paths.
- Safe to show: public portfolio screenshots, synthetic teacher workspace examples, public project name, and plain launch-surface labels.
