# Design

## Style Prompt

Use a warm product-build case-study style that fits Palabruno's Spanish-learning surface: off-white classroom paper, ink text, burnt-orange brand accents, sage and soft blue supporting accents, crisp product screenshots, and restrained editorial motion. The video should feel practical and founder-facing, not like a generic SaaS promo. Keep copy plain, short, and anchored in the simple story: founder idea, first buyer, product shape, teacher workflow, launch result, and market test.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#F7F3EF`
- Paper: `#FFFCF8`
- Ink: `#1F1F1D`
- Muted text: `#6F6962`
- Border: `#D8CEC6`
- Brand: `#A8552F`
- Brand dark: `#8D4326`
- Brand soft: `#F4DDD2`
- Sage: `#256245`
- Blue note: `#315A92`
- Dark frame: `#1F1A17`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, serif
- Interface/body: `Avenir Next`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif
- Data/labels: `SF Mono`, `Menlo`, monospace

## Motion

- Medium-energy editorial explainer pacing.
- Use push slides for adjacent story points and blur crossfades for the solution reveal and final wind-down.
- Every scene enters with varied opacity, transform, and rule/card reveals.
- Keep product screenshots steady after entry so the viewer can inspect them.
- Use native cards only for real story structure: audiences, product surfaces, workflow actions, and launch surfaces.
- Keep ambient motion subtle: slow background paper shifts, small rule reveals, and screenshot parallax.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, or generic dark SaaS styling.
- Do not make the whole video beige/orange; balance the warm brand with ink, sage, and blue accents.
- Do not show implementation stack names, schema names, API names, test output, or command output on screen.
- Do not zoom into student names, account footer text, billing details, or any private runtime state.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
- Do not let decorative cards compete with product screenshots.
