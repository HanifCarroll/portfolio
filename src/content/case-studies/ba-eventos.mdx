<CaseSection title="Summary">
	Buenos Aires event information is scattered across Instagram stories, Facebook events, Eventbrite, Passline, venue sites, and WhatsApp groups.

    I built BA Eventos as a live discovery product that turns that fragmented local data into a searchable experience with grounded AI answers.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Workflow Objective">
	Traditional search does not work well for events. People ask for moods, neighborhoods, time windows, and social context, not just exact keywords.

    The product needed a data pipeline, an admin surface, and a retrieval layer that could answer those questions without hallucinating dead-end recommendations.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="System Design">
	<DecisionBlock title="Turned messy sources into a usable event dataset.">
		The system ingests events, matches venues, enriches descriptions, and keeps the catalog organized enough to support real search.
	</DecisionBlock>

    <DecisionBlock title="Combined semantic search with structured filters.">
    	Users can ask intent-heavy questions like “romantic jazz in Palermo” or “underground techno this weekend” while the system stays grounded in event records.
    </DecisionBlock>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Product Proof" media>
	The product is a working example of AI-assisted discovery over messy local data, not just a chat interface.

    <ImagePlate image={baEventosFeature} alt="BA Eventos event discovery product interface." />

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Results">
	BA Eventos launched with 800+ events and 190+ venues pulled into one searchable experience.

    The project proved a repeatable pattern for AI discovery products: ingest the data, clean it, enrich it, embed it, and answer with grounded results.

</CaseSection>
