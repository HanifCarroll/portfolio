<CaseSection title="Summary">
	Casa Elaria needed a way to test purchase intent before spending on inventory.

    I built a convincing fake-door storefront so the founders could learn which products attracted real interest and collect early demand before committing capital.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Business Objective">
	The risk was not whether a storefront could be built. The real risk was buying inventory before knowing whether people would try to buy.

    The first version needed to feel real enough to test intent while staying honest about the product not being ready to ship yet.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="The Work">
	<DecisionBlock title="Built the buying moment without taking payment.">
		The storefront guided visitors through a normal e-commerce flow, then captured email interest at the final step instead of charging at checkout.
	</DecisionBlock>

    <DecisionBlock title="Turned curiosity into an early launch list.">
    	The test gave the founders a way to compare product interest and build a warmer audience before spending on inventory.
    </DecisionBlock>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Site Proof" media>
	The page needed to create enough belief for visitors to behave like buyers, not just casual browsers.

    <ImagePlate image={casaElariaFeature} alt="Casa Elaria fake-door e-commerce storefront." />

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Results">
	Instead of guessing, the founders got a low-risk signal on which products drew the strongest interest.

    The test also built an early launch list, which gave them proof of demand and a warmer audience before they spent on inventory.

</CaseSection>
