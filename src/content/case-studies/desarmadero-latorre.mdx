<CaseSection title="Summary">
	The business had a large, changing auto-parts inventory, but customers had to call or visit to check what was available.

    The work turned that phone-heavy process into a searchable digital stockroom that customers could browse before contacting the team.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Workflow Objective">
	Staff were spending too much time answering repetitive availability questions. Customers were also arriving with vague requests because they had no way to inspect stock themselves.

    The goal was to make inventory visible enough that inbound questions became more qualified and less dependent on constant phone time.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="System Design">
	<DecisionBlock title="Made stock searchable before the first call.">
		I built a customer-facing catalog with search by make, model, and year so buyers could narrow the question before reaching out.
	</DecisionBlock>

    <DecisionBlock title="Kept the staff workflow simple.">
    	The internal workflow focused on adding vehicles, photos, and basic listing information without forcing the business into a heavy inventory system it would not maintain.
    </DecisionBlock>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Workflow Proof" media>
	The useful shift was not just a nicer website. It was giving customers a way to self-qualify before staff got involved.

    <ImagePlate image={desarmaderoFeature} alt="Desarmadero Latorre searchable inventory interface." />

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Results">
	Customers can now search the catalog before calling, which turns vague stock questions into more qualified inquiries and frees staff from repeating the same availability checks all day.

    The business also gained a 24/7 way to surface inventory without relying on phone time alone.

</CaseSection>
