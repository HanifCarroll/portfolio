<CaseSection title="Summary">
	Mucho Hangouts was live, but reliability and team velocity were becoming business risks.

    As fractional CTO, I helped stabilize core product foundations while the team kept shipping.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Business Objective">
	Messaging, notifications, and release flow were fragile enough to slow product work and create recurring user frustration.

    The team needed stronger development patterns and clearer technical direction without pausing the product.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="The Work">
	<DecisionBlock title="Stabilized the product while delivery continued.">
		I focused on clearer API boundaries, more reliable real-time behavior, and patterns junior developers could extend safely.
	</DecisionBlock>

    <DecisionBlock title="Reduced dependency on ad hoc senior fixes.">
    	Code review, pairing, and day-to-day technical direction helped the team ship with less hand-holding and fewer regressions.
    </DecisionBlock>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Product Proof" media>
	The product needed stronger foundations so everyday social coordination would feel dependable to users.

    <ImagePlate image={muchoHangoutsFeature} alt="Mucho Hangouts social events product interface." />

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Results">
	Messaging and notifications became dependable enough for day-to-day use instead of a recurring source of user frustration.

    The team ended up with a more reliable product, clearer patterns for new work, and junior developers who could ship with less hand-holding.

</CaseSection>
