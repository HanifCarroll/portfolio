<CaseSection title="Summary">
	Vox Prismatic turned long-form transcripts into a reviewable content operations system.

    The product reduced the bottleneck between raw material and scheduled posts while keeping a human in control before publishing.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Business Objective">
	Content teams can record hours of valuable material, then lose momentum because the useful ideas stay buried in long transcripts.

    The product needed to extract useful moments, preserve voice, and make the publishing workflow operational instead of producing generic AI drafts.

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="The Work">
	<DecisionBlock title="Turned transcripts into reviewable post drafts.">
		The pipeline chunks long transcripts, extracts candidate insights, and turns them into drafts someone can review instead of mining the transcript by hand.
	</DecisionBlock>

    <DecisionBlock title="Made the workflow operational.">
    	Hook frameworks, scheduling controls, timezone-aware publishing, and progress updates made the system feel like a tool, not just a generator.
    </DecisionBlock>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Product Proof" media>
	The product shows a repeatable pattern for AI-assisted content operations where voice control, long inputs, and scheduling reliability all matter.

    <ImagePlate image={voxPrismaticFeature} alt="Vox Prismatic AI content transformation interface." />

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection title="Results">
	The workflow now extracts insights, drafts posts, and keeps a human in control before anything gets scheduled.

    It proved a useful pattern for turning raw expertise into publishable material without handing the whole process to a generic content generator.

</CaseSection>
